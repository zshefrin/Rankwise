How much does HVAC marketing cost in British Columbia?

HVAC marketing in BC typically runs between $500 and $3,000 per month, with most contractors spending $800 to $1,800. What you get varies widely — a $500 package is usually Google Business Profile management and not much else, while $1,500+ typically covers local SEO, content, reviews, and ongoing optimization across multiple platforms.

According to Google's Think with Google data, 76% of people who conduct a local search on their smartphone visit a business within 24 hours. That visibility is what HVAC marketing actually buys — a spot in the first three Map Pack results when a homeowner in your service area searches "furnace repair near me."

What do you get at each price point?

$500–$800/month. GBP post scheduling, review responses, and maybe a monthly blog post. You're paying someone to keep the lights on, not to grow the business. Appropriate if you already rank well and just need consistency.

$800–$1,500/month. Adds local SEO — citation building, GBP category optimization, on-site SEO for your website. This is where the math starts to work: new rankings generate calls, calls generate jobs.

$1,500–$3,000/month. Full-stack local marketing — SEO, content, GBP, social, reporting, and strategy. Usually includes a dedicated point of contact. Fits contractors with 3+ techs who need the phone ringing reliably across Vancouver, Burnaby, and the North Shore.

$3,000+/month. Typically includes paid media (Google Ads), advanced tracking, and multi-location management. The extra spend is mostly ad budget, not agency fees.

Why does HVAC marketing cost more than generic small-business marketing?

Three reasons:

1. Local intent is more competitive. HVAC searches in Metro Vancouver are crowded — established contractors have spent years building citation profiles and review counts. Catching up takes real work. 2. Google Business Profile is the main battleground. Unlike e-commerce SEO, HVAC traffic funnels through the Map Pack. Ranking there requires consistent GBP activity, photos, posts, and review response — not just on-page SEO. 3. Service areas complicate targeting. A contractor serving Vancouver, Burnaby, Richmond, and the North Shore needs geographic content and citations for each — not just the head-office city.

Is SEO cheaper than Google Ads for HVAC contractors?

Long-term, yes. Google Ads for "furnace repair Vancouver" and similar terms typically run $10–$30 per click in Metro Vancouver, and HVAC lead quality varies. SEO has higher upfront effort but compounds — rankings earned in month 4 keep generating leads in month 24 at no incremental cost.

Most contractors end up running both: Ads for immediate lead flow, SEO for durable visibility.

How long before I see a return?

Realistically, 3–6 months for SEO to show meaningful traction. GBP optimization can move rankings in weeks if the profile was underoptimized to start. Anyone promising top-3 rankings in 30 days is either lying or planning to use tactics that Google will eventually penalize.

What should I pay attention to when comparing agencies?

Five things:

  • Do they show you monthly rank reports? If not, you have no way to verify they're doing anything.
  • Do they rank well themselves? If their own Google Business Profile is invisible, they can't rank yours.
  • Are contracts month-to-month or locked in? Long lock-ins protect the agency, not you.
  • Do they understand HVAC specifically? Generic agencies waste months learning your vocabulary and service areas.
  • What does their content actually look like? Read three of their posts. If they sound like every other agency, that's what you'll get.
