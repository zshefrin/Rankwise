How Do I Get More HVAC Leads in Vancouver?

The most direct answer: get your Google Business Profile ranking in the Map Pack for searches like "furnace repair Vancouver" or "heat pump installation Surrey," then back it up with a website that converts those clicks into calls. For HVAC contractors in Metro Vancouver, Google local search is where the majority of inbound leads come from — not referrals, not social media, not flyers.

According to BrightLocal's 2023 Local Consumer Review Survey, 98% of consumers used the internet to find a local business in the past year, and Google is the dominant platform. For trade services like HVAC, that intent is high — someone searching "furnace repair Burnaby" is ready to book, not browse.

Why Are HVAC Contractors Hard to Find on Google Maps?

Most HVAC contractors in Metro Vancouver have a Google Business Profile. Most of those profiles are incomplete, stale, or miscategorized — which means Google doesn't surface them when it matters.

The Map Pack (the three local results that appear above organic search) captures the majority of clicks on any local service query. If your profile isn't in those three spots, you're competing for the scraps below. The contractors ranking there aren't doing anything mysterious — they're maintaining active, complete profiles while everyone else set it and forgot it.

Specific problems that push HVAC profiles down:

  • Wrong or too-broad primary category (e.g., "contractor" instead of "HVAC contractor")
  • No posts in six-plus months
  • Fewer than 10 photos
  • Unanswered reviews — positive or negative
  • Service areas that don't match where you actually work

What Actually Brings in More HVAC Calls from Google?

Two things move the needle for HVAC contractors in Vancouver: GBP activity and local SEO on your website. They work together — the GBP gets you into the Map Pack; the website handles the searchers who click through to learn more before calling.

On the GBP side:

  • Post once a week — a completed job, a tip about heat pump efficiency, a seasonal service reminder. Google tracks freshness.
  • Respond to every review within 48 hours. According to Google's own guidance, responding to reviews improves your local ranking and signals that the business is active.
  • Add job photos consistently. Google's data shows businesses with more photos receive more direction requests and website clicks than those without.
  • Fill in every field: services, service area, business description, attributes. Every blank field is a missed ranking signal.

On the website side:

  • You need landing pages targeting city-specific queries: "furnace repair Vancouver," "heat pump installation Richmond," "AC service Surrey." One generic services page doesn't rank for all of those.
  • Page titles and headers should match how homeowners actually search — not trade terminology.
  • Your phone number needs to be prominent and clickable on mobile. Most HVAC searches happen on a phone.

How Long Does It Take to Get More HVAC Leads from SEO?

Faster than most contractors expect for the GBP, slower for organic rankings.

| Timeframe | What changes | |---|---| | 0–30 days | GBP improvements take effect — more people see your listing, more profile clicks | | 60–90 days | Target keyword rankings improve; new leads from homeowners who've never heard of you | | 6+ months | Consistent, predictable inbound — stops being feast-or-famine |

The GBP is the fastest lever. A neglected profile that gets properly optimized and starts receiving weekly posts and review responses will typically see measurable improvement in search appearances within the first month. Organic ranking for competitive terms like "HVAC contractor Vancouver" takes longer — 60 to 90 days is realistic for meaningful movement, assuming the work is done correctly from the start.

Don't trust anyone who promises top-3 rankings by a specific date. Google doesn't work on a schedule, and anyone claiming otherwise is selling you something.

Does Getting More Google Reviews Actually Help HVAC Rankings?

Yes — reviews are one of the stronger local ranking signals Google uses, and they directly influence whether homeowners call you or the next contractor.

Moz's Local Search Ranking Factors research consistently identifies review signals — quantity, velocity, and diversity — as meaningful inputs into Map Pack rankings. For HVAC specifically, homeowners are making a trust decision before they call. A contractor with 11 reviews and a 4.8 average is going to get the call over a competitor with 3 reviews and no responses, even if the rankings are otherwise similar.

The practical system: ask every satisfied customer before you leave the job site. A direct ask converts far better than an email two days later. Keep it simple — "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It helps a lot." Most will do it on the spot.
